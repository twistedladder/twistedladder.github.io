About the Event

The 5th annual Student Symposium is hosted by Chevron Corporation and will be held at Chevron’s 1500 Louisiana Street office in downtown Houston, TX on February 4th-6th, 2016.

The theme of this year’s conference, “The Road to US Oil and Gas Independence,” will highlight the various challenges associated with producing unconventionals, and running successful operations in the current price market. The Student Symposium will serve as an opportunity for students to meet colleagues from universities across the United States and expand their knowledge of the oil and gas industry.

Throughout the conference, students will to not only listen to keynote speeches and panels sessions featuring industry experts, but will also have the chance to take part in workshops and round table talks to help provide a holistic view of the industry, and increase the amount of networking opportunities.

Corporate Sponsorship

The SPE chapters of The University of Texas at Austin and The University of Oklahoma are organizing the 5th annual Student Symposium (formerly known as Student Summit) hosted by Chevron. The conference will take place in Houston, Texas, at the Chevron Center from February 4-6, 2016.

Student Symposium Objectives:

  • Present an opportunity for students to enhance their knowledge of the oil and gas industry through the insights and experiences of industry leaders and advocates
  • Focus on encouraging future energy leaders' interest in learning as much as possible about all apsects of the oil and gas industry
  • Provide the attendants with a myriad of networking opportunities, as students, faculty, and industry experts from around the country and across the globe will be participating

    • The 2016 Student Syposium's theme will be "The Road to US Energy Independence." The lectures, panel sessions, and exhibition will expand upon the growth of the oil and gas industry and how, through innovation, research, and growth, the United States is becoming self-sufficient with regards to energy consumption. The conference will address shale development and challenges under the low-price environment. The invitation is open to petroleum engineering students and related energy majors across the United States. We expect the attendance to be approximately 320 students from 20 universities and 20 speakers during the three-day conference.
